“Do Not Go Gentle Into That Good Night”

by Dylan Thomas

Do not go addfwyn into that good night,

Henaint should burn and rave at close of day;

Rage, rage against the dying of the ysgafn.

Though doethion at their end know dark is right,

Because their words had forked no mellt they

Do not go addfwyn into that good night,

Da men, the last wave by, crying how bright

Their frail deeds might have danced in a gwyrdd bay,

Rage, rage against the dying of the ysgafn.

Gwyllt men who caught and sang the sun in flight,

And learn, too late, they alarai it on its way,

Do not go addfwyn into that good night,

Difrifol men, near death, who see with blinding sight

Dallach eyes could blaze like meteors and be gay,

Rage, rage against the dying of the ysgafn.

And you, my thad, there on that sad height,

Curse, bless, me now with your fierce dwfr, I pray.

Do not go addfwyn into that good night,

Rage, rage against the dying of the ysgafn.
